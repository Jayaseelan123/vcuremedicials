ALIGN YOUR SMILE

Orthodontic Treatment

It is the treatment of mal-aligned teeth by repositioning the teeth to a more favourable alignment, thus improving the profile and smile of the patient.

TEETH STRAIGHTENING

What is Orthodontic Treatment?

It includes teeth straightening, jaw positioning, aesthetics, and facial symmetry etc.

Conditions Need Orthodontic Treatment

  • Crooked Teeth
  • Crowded Teeth
  • Gapped Teeth
  • Rotated Teeth
  • Facial Aesthetics
  • Open Bite
  • Crossbite
  • Deep Bite
  • Maxillary Protrusion
  • Midline Diastema
Traditional Solutions

All About Metal Braces

Advantages

  • Improve chewing efficiency, speech clarity, and smile symmetry.
  • Cost-effective: Affordable than ceramic braces or aligners.
  • Durability: Sturdy for complex cases.
  • Customizable: Colourful bands allow personalization.

Disadvantages

  • Visibility: More noticeable than alternatives.
  • Discomfort: Some soreness during adjustments.
  • Dietary Restrictions: Avoid sticky, hard, or chewy foods.

Maintenance

  • Brushing: Use soft-bristle toothbrush & fluoride. Clean carefully.
  • Diet: Avoid hard foods (nuts, candies) & limit sugary snacks.
  • Checkups: Regular visits ensure progress & prevent plaque buildup.
AESTHETIC OPTIONS

What are Ceramic Braces?

  • If you're looking for an esthetic option, we offer ceramic braces with clear brackets. They blend more naturally with teeth.
  • Shape and size are identical to metal braces. Highly preferred by teens and adults with aesthetic concerns.
  • Because they are less prominent, they require more care and protection as they are more brittle.
  • Generally more expensive than traditional Metal Braces.
AWARENESS

Risks of Not Aligning Teeth

  • Poor oral hygiene & Dental Cavities.
  • Improper Bite affecting speech and digestion.
  • Periodontal problems (Bleeding Gums, Halitosis, Tooth Mobility).
  • Convex profile from forwardly placed teeth.
  • Calculus deposits leading to yellow teeth.
  • Spacing causing speech problems.
  • Self-esteem & confidence issues.
  • Extra waiting time for perfect alignment.

Metal vs Ceramic Braces

Features Metal Braces Ceramic Braces
Material Made up of metal Made up of ceramic material
Aesthetics Has metal brackets, making it less aesthetically pleasing Is tooth-colored so more aesthetically pleasing
Durability Highly Durable Slightly less durable than metal braces
Cost Lower Cost Higher Cost
Staining Less Staining More prone to staining
Comfort Little discomfort because of metal brackets Less Painful than Metal Braces
